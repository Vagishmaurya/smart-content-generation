🌟 Welcome to "Reach for the Top"

This chapter brings us two inspiring biographical accounts of remarkable individuals who reached the pinnacle of success in their respective fields. It shows us what determination, hard work, and perseverance can achieve.

In this interactive lesson, we'll explore the journeys of Santosh Yadav, the first woman to scale Mt Everest twice, and Maria Sharapova, who rose to become the world's top-ranked tennis player at a young age. We'll also study the poem "On Killing a Tree" by Gieve Patel, which explores our relationship with nature.

📝 Let's Begin

Before we read about these remarkable individuals who reached the top in their chosen fields, let's think about people we admire for their achievements.

Think for a while and make a list of three to five persons you idolize or admire very much for their achievements. Your idols may be from any sphere of life — sports, medicine, media, or art and culture.

Pre-Reading Activity: My Role Models

Take a moment to reflect on the people you admire. What qualities do they possess? What challenges did they overcome? What makes their achievements special to you?

❓ Reading Comprehension Questions

🔍 Clauses

Read these sentences taken from the text:

I reached the market when most of the shops had closed.

This sentence has two clauses. The part "I reached the market" is one clause, and "when most of the shops had closed" is another clause that tells us when the first action happened.

Match the clauses to what they tell us:

1. When Rahul Dravid walked back towards the pavilion, everyone stood up.

2. The telephone rang and Ganga picked it up.

3. Gunjan has been with us ever since the school began.

📚 Combining Sentences

In this exercise, you will combine pairs of sentences into one sentence using appropriate connecting words.

Combine these pairs of sentences into one sentence:

1. Grandfather told me about the old days. All books were printed on paper then.

2. What do you do after you finish the book? Perhaps you just throw it away.

3. He gave the little girl an apple. He took the computer apart.

4. You have nothing. That makes you very determined.

5. I never thought of quitting. I knew what I wanted.
